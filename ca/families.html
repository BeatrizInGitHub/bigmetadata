Families¶

Familial arrangements of people and households.

Columns¶

  • Total number of census families in private households (6 subcolumns)
    • Size of census family: 2 persons
    • Size of census family: 3 persons
    • Size of census family: 4 persons
    • Size of census family: 5 or more persons
    • Total couple families by family structure and number of children (2 subcolumns)
    • Total lone-parent families by sex of parent and number of children (2 subcolumns)
  • Total children in census families in private households (6 subcolumns)
    • Under six years of age
    • 6 to 14 years
    • 15 to 17 years
    • 18 to 24 years
    • 25 years and over
    • Average number of children at home per census family

    • Married or living with a common-law partner (female) (2 subcolumns)
    • Not married and not living with a common-law partner (female) (4 subcolumns)
  • Total population 15 years and over by marital status (male) (2 subcolumns)
    • Married or living with a common-law partner (male) (2 subcolumns)
    • Not married and not living with a common-law partner (male) (4 subcolumns)
  • Total population 15 years and over by marital status (total) (2 subcolumns)
    • Married or living with a common-law partner (total) (2 subcolumns)
    • Not married and not living with a common-law partner (total) (4 subcolumns)
  • Total population in private households (female) (3 subcolumns)
    • Total population - First generation (female)
    • Total population - Second generation (female)
    • Total population - Third generation or more (female)
  • Total population in private households (male) (3 subcolumns)

  • Total population in private households (total) (3 subcolumns)

    • Total population - Second generation (total)
    • Total population - Third generation or more (total)
